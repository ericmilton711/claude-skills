## Full Speech (final draft)

Imagine standing in a crowd of 81,000 fans. The ground is shaking under your feet. An entire wall of yellow is singing in unison, so loud you can feel it in your chest. That is what it feels like inside Signal Iduna Park, home of Borussia Dortmund. Going to BVB games in Dortmund changed the way I see soccer forever, and today I want to tell you how that happened.

I ended up in Dortmund because of a girl. Koko was an old high school girlfriend of mine who was German. She lived in a town called Hagen, just north of Dortmund. When I went to visit her, it was actually her friends who invited me to a Borussia Dortmund match. I didn't know much about the team. I didn't know much about German soccer at all. But I figured, why not.

What blew me away before the game even started was that Koko's friend showed me we could just walk right onto the pitch. No security making a big deal out of it. No one stopping us. No one even looking twice. Coming from the U.S., where you can't get within 50 feet of a field without someone checking your credentials, that was pretty unreal. And on top of that, the tickets were only 15 Euros. Fifteen Euros to get into a stadium that holds over 80,000 people. I couldn't believe it.

Then the match started. And I saw the Yellow Wall for the first time. The Sudtribune. Over 25,000 fans packed into one stand, all dressed in yellow and black, all singing together. Not just cheering — singing. The chants would start on one side and roll across the entire stadium like a wave. The noise was constant. It never stopped. It was unlike anything I had ever experienced at a sporting event. I've been to NFL games, NBA games, but nothing came close to that atmosphere.

After that first game, I was hooked. I kept going to matches with Koko's friends. I learned the chants. I followed the players. I became part of that community. BVB wasn't just a team I watched anymore. It became something I belonged to.

What started as a casual outing with my girlfriend's friends became one of the most defining experiences of my life. I never expected a 15 Euro ticket to change the way I see a sport, but it did. Now, every time I watch a BVB match, no matter where I am in the world, I'm right back in that stadium, surrounded by 81,000 fans, singing along with the Yellow Wall.
